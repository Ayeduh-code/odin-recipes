Matcha cheesecake

A rich but earthy cheesecake that is a delight to the tastebuds

This recipe is one that I took from a site online and tweaked when I messed up preparing it originally. The consistency is a bit more loose than the japanese cheesecake but less dense than a New York Cheescake Overall, the cheesecake went over well and it was a good learning experience.

If you are looking for a cheesecake that is more matcha flavor than other matcha cheesecakes you've tried, then this recipe is for you.

Ingredients you'll need

  • 2 x 8oz packages of fat-free cream cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of matcha powder
  • 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract (to taste)
  • 1/4 cup of sour cream
  • 10 graham cracker sheets for the crust
  • 6 tablespoons of salted butter for the crust

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350F.
  2. Make the crust by crushing 10 graham cracker sheets until it is a crumb like consistency.
  3. Melt 8 tablespoons of butter and then combine with the graham cracker sheets.
  4. Line a 8-inch springform pan and compress with the bottom of a glass until it coats the bottom of the pan and goes up the sides a little.
  5. In a separate bowl combine the room temperature cream cheese and sugar until well combined.
  6. To the cream cheese mixture, add eggs one at a time. Mix after adding each egg until well incorporated.
  7. Add the matcha powder, vanilla, and sour cream to the mixture and beat until smooth.
  8. Pour batter into springform pan with graham cracker crust.
  9. Level the top of the cheesecake and wrap the bottom of the pan with aluminum foil and plastic cling film
  10. Add water to baking sheet and place cheesecake until water bath
  11. Bake until the center of the cheesecake is mostly set but has a slight jiggle. (usually around 25 minutes). For the last 10 minutes, cover the top with aluminum foil to prevent excessive browning on the top.
  12. Let cool for at least 1-2 hours before serving.
    • Optional: Add whipped cream to each slice when serving.
